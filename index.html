Model Interview Answers

Don't memorize—internalize the concepts.

Technical Risk Concepts

Q1: How do you define Risk to a non-technical board member?

The Trap: Do not use technical jargon like "CVE" or "buffer overflow".

"I define risk as the potential for loss or harm to our business goals. It’s simply the likelihood of a bad event happening multiplied by the impact it would have on our revenue, reputation, or operations. My job is to translate technical threats into business language so we can decide how much insurance to buy or how many locks to put on the doors."

Q2: What is the difference between Inherent and Residual Risk?

Inherent Risk: The raw risk level before controls (walking in the rain without an umbrella).

Residual Risk: What remains after controls are applied (walking in rain WITH an umbrella). You stay mostly dry, but there is still a small risk your shoes get wet. Management decides if that residual risk is acceptable.

Frameworks & Processes

Q4: Walk me through a Third-Party Risk Assessment (TPRM).

  1. Categorization: Determine data access (PII/Critical vs. Low).
  2. Due Diligence: Send questionnaire (SIG-Lite) or request SOC 2 Type II report.
  3. Review: Analyze controls (Encryption? MFA? Incident Response?).
  4. Risk Scoring: Assign a risk score.
  5. Contracting: Ensure 'Right to Audit' and SLAs are in the contract.

Q5: We have a critical vulnerability on a legacy server that cannot be patched. What do you do?

The Trap: Never say "Force the patch" (breaks business) or "Accept the risk" (negligent).

"We implement Compensating Controls:"

  • Isolation: Air-gap the server or strict VLAN.
  • Monitoring: Increase alert sensitivity for this host.
  • Virtual Patching: Use WAF to block exploits at network level.

"Finally, document the Exception in the Risk Register with a review date."

Situational & Behavioral

Q6: A PM wants to launch tomorrow, but you found a High-Risk vulnerability. Delay costs $100k. What do you do?

"I am not the 'Department of No'; I am the 'Department of How'. I would immediately quantify the risk. If the vulnerability allows a breach costing $5M, the $100k delay is worth it. I present this to the Business Owner. If they sign off on Accepting the Risk formally, I allow the launch but ensure they accept liability, and I prioritize a hotfix immediately."

Buzzword Dictionary

Speak the language.

RTO / RPO

Recovery Time Objective: How long can we be down?
Recovery Point Objective: How much data can we lose?

SOC 2 Type II

The gold standard report for vendor security. "Type II" means it proves controls worked over a period of time (6-12 months).

Zero Trust

"Never trust, always verify." Security model requiring identity verification for every person and device trying to access resources.

Risk Appetite

The amount and type of risk that an organization is willing to take in order to meet their strategic objectives.

SIEM

Security Information and Event Management. The software system where logs go to be analyzed for threats.

KRI vs KPI

KRI: Future-looking (Warning signals).
KPI: Backward-looking (Performance metrics).

You've got this. Risk is not about eliminating danger; it's about managing it so the business can grow.
